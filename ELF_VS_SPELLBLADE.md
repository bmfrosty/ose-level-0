# Elf vs Spellblade Comparison

This document compares the OSE Standard **Elf** class with the Gygar Mode **Spellblade** class.

## Overview

Both classes combine martial prowess with arcane spellcasting, but they differ significantly in their implementation and power level.

## Key Differences

### Race vs Class

**OSE Elf:**
- Demihuman race-as-class
- Only elves can be this class
- Minimum INT 9 required

**Gygar Spellblade:**
- Pure class (not race-locked)
- Both humans and elves can become spellblades
- Humans require STR 13+ and INT 13+
- Elves require STR 9+ and INT 9+

### Level Progression

| Aspect | OSE Elf | Gygar Spellblade |
|--------|---------|------------------|
| **Maximum Level** | 10 | 10 |
| **Hit Dice** | 1d6 | 1d6 |
| **XP to Level 2** | 4,000 | 4,000 |
| **XP to Level 10** | 600,000 | 600,000 |

Both classes have identical XP requirements and hit dice progression.

### Combat Capabilities

**OSE Elf:**
- Can use all weapons and armour
- No special combat restrictions
- THAC0 progression: 19 [0] at levels 1-3, 17 [+2] at 4-6, 14 [+5] at 7-9, 12 [+7] at level 10
- **Irregular progression** - jumps at levels 4, 7, and 10

**Gygar Spellblade:**
- Can use all weapons and any armor type
- Heavy armor may interfere with spellcasting (referee's ruling)
- To-Hit Bonus: **Smooth linear progression** from +1 to +7
- Increases every level or two: +1 (L1), +2 (L2), +3 (L3), +4 (L4), +5 (L5-6), +6 (L7-8), +7 (L9-10)

**KEY DIFFERENCE:** The Spellblade has a **smoothed-out, more consistent** to-hit progression compared to the Elf's irregular jumps.

### Spellcasting

**OSE Elf:**
- Uses magic-user spell list
- Spell progression at level 10: 3/3/3/3/2 (1st through 5th level spells)
- Can research spells at any level
- Can create magic items at 9th level

**Gygar Spellblade:**
- Uses magic-user spell list (or custom list per referee)
- Spell progression at level 10: 3/3/3/3/2 (1st through 5th level spells)
- Can research spells, craft items, or perform arcane experiments at 9th level

Both have identical spell progression.

### Special Abilities

**OSE Elf has several racial abilities:**
- Detect secret doors (2-in-6)
- Immunity to ghoul paralysis
- Infravision 60'
- Listen at doors (2-in-6)

**Gygar Spellblade:**
- No special racial abilities (it's a class, not a race)
- If the spellblade is an elf, they would have elf racial abilities separately

### Prime Requisites & XP Bonuses

**OSE Elf:**
- Prime requisites: INT and STR
- +5% XP bonus if both INT and STR are 13+
- +10% XP bonus if INT 16+ and STR 13+

**Gygar Spellblade:**
- Prime requisites: STR and INT
- +5% XP bonus if both STR and INT are 13+

### High-Level Benefits (9th-10th Level)

**OSE Elf:**
- Can build a forest stronghold at 9th level
- Forest animals within 5 miles become friends
- Can only hire elven mercenaries
- Specialists and retainers of any race may be hired

**Gygar Spellblade:**
- Can establish a stronghold at 9th level
- Followers, students, or warriors may come to train
- No racial restrictions on hiring

### Saving Throws

**MAJOR DIFFERENCE:** The classes have **significantly different** saving throw progressions.

**OSE Elf (Irregular Progression):**

| Level | Death | Wands | Paralysis | Breath | Spells |
|-------|-------|-------|-----------|--------|--------|
| 1-3   | 12    | 13    | 13        | 15     | 15     |
| 4-6   | 10    | 11    | 11        | 13     | 12     |
| 7-9   | 8     | 9     | 9         | 10     | 10     |
| 10    | 6     | 7     | 8         | 8      | 8      |

**Gygar Spellblade (Smoothed Progression):**

| Level | Death | Wands | Paralysis | Breath | Spells |
|-------|-------|-------|-----------|--------|--------|
| 1     | 12    | 13    | 13        | 15     | 15     |
| 2     | 12    | 13    | 13        | 15     | 14     |
| 3     | 11    | 12    | 12        | 14     | 13     |
| 4     | 10    | 11    | 11        | 13     | 12     |
| 5     | 10    | 11    | 11        | 12     | 12     |
| 6     | 9     | 10    | 10        | 11     | 11     |
| 7     | 8     | 9     | 9         | 10     | 10     |
| 8     | 8     | 9     | 9         | 10     | 10     |
| 9     | 7     | 8     | 9         | 9      | 9      |
| 10    | 6     | 7     | 8         | 8      | 8      |

**KEY DIFFERENCE:** The Spellblade has **more frequent, gradual improvements** in saving throws (nearly every level) compared to the Elf's larger jumps at levels 4, 7, and 10. The Spellblade ends with identical saves at level 10 (Death 6, Wands 7, Paralysis 8, Breath 8, Spells 8), but gets there through a smoother progression curve.

## Summary

### OSE Elf Advantages:
- Built-in racial abilities (detect secret doors, ghoul immunity, infravision)
- Slightly better XP bonus potential (+10% vs +5%)
- Thematic integration of race and class

### Gygar Spellblade Advantages:
- Available to both humans and elves
- More flexible character concept (class not tied to race)
- Clearer separation of race and class mechanics
- Potentially easier requirements for elves (STR 9+ vs no minimum)
- **Smoother, more consistent to-hit progression** (linear improvement vs irregular jumps)
- **Smoother saving throw progression** (more frequent improvements, same final values at level 10)

### Mechanical Similarities:
- Same XP progression
- Same hit dice
- Same spell progression

## Conclusion

The **Gygar Spellblade** was created by taking the OSE Elf class and making three key changes:

1. **Removed racial abilities** - Stripped out detect secret doors, ghoul immunity, and infravision to make it a pure class rather than race-as-class
2. **Smoothed to-hit progression** - Changed from irregular jumps (at levels 4, 7, 10) to a consistent linear progression every 1-2 levels
3. **Smoothed saving throw progression** - Changed from large jumps every 3-4 levels to gradual improvements nearly every level, reaching the same final values at level 10

The result is a **mechanically smoother** class with more consistent progression in combat and survivability, but without the utility of racial abilities. This design allows:
- **Humans** to become spellblades (gaining access to the fighter-mage archetype)
- **Elves** to choose other classes (fighter, thief, cleric, magic-user, or spellblade)
- **Cleaner separation** between race and class mechanics in Gygar Mode

In essence, the Spellblade is the Elf class **refined and balanced** for use as a standalone class option rather than a racial class.
