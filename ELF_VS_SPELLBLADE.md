# Elf vs Spellblade Comparison (Gygar Mode)

This document compares the Elf (race-as-class) and Spellblade (pure class) options in Gygar Mode.

## Overview

Both Elf and Spellblade are warrior-mages who combine martial prowess with arcane spellcasting. The key difference is that Elf is a race-as-class with racial abilities, while Spellblade is a pure class without racial features.

---

## Identical Mechanics

### Progressions
- **Hit Dice:** Both use 1d6 per level until 9th level
- **Maximum Level:** Both reach level 10
- **To-Hit Progression:** Identical Fighter progression (+1 to +7)
- **Saving Throws:** Identical progression tables
- **Spell Progression:** Identical spell slots per level (1st-5th level spells)
- **XP Requirements:** Identical XP needed per level

### Combat
- **Armor:** Both can use any armor and shields
- **Weapons:** Both can use any weapons

### Magic
- **Spell List:** Both use the magic-user spell list
- **Spell Casting:** Both prepare spells from spellbooks
- **Magical Research:** Both can research spells at any level
- **Magic Items:** Both can create magic items at 9th level
- **Scroll Use:** Both can use arcane scrolls

### XP Bonuses
- **Both:** +5% if INT and STR 13+, +10% if INT 16+ and STR 13+

---

## Key Differences

### Requirements

**Elf:**
- Must be an elf (race requirement)
- Minimum INT 9

**Spellblade:**
- Human or Elf
- Humans require STR and INT 13+
- Elves require STR and INT 9+

### Racial Abilities

**Elf has these racial abilities:**
- **Detect Secret Doors:** 2-in-6 chance when searching
- **Immunity to Ghoul Paralysis:** Cannot be paralyzed by ghouls
- **Infravision:** 60' range
- **Listening at Doors:** 2-in-6 chance

**Spellblade has:**
- No racial abilities (pure class)

### Stronghold (9th Level)

**Elf:**
- Constructs a stronghold in the depths of the forest
- Forest animals within 5 miles become friends
- Must protect the animals from harm
- May only hire elven mercenaries (specialists/retainers of any race)

**Spellblade:**
- Establishes a stronghold (no specific location requirement)
- Followers, students, or warriors may come to train
- No restrictions on mercenary hiring

---

## Which to Choose?

### Choose Elf if you want:
- Racial abilities (detect doors, ghoul immunity, infravision, listen at doors)
- To play specifically as an elf with elven heritage and culture
- A forest-themed stronghold with animal allies
- The classic race-as-class experience

### Choose Spellblade if you want:
- A pure class without racial restrictions
- To play as a human warrior-mage
- More flexibility in stronghold location and followers
- To focus purely on the warrior-scholar concept without racial baggage

### For Elven Characters:
- **Elf:** Better if you want the racial abilities and elven flavor
- **Spellblade:** Better if you want easier requirements (INT 9+ instead of needing specific stats) and don't care about racial abilities

---

## Summary

**Mechanically Identical:** Both classes have the same combat capability, spellcasting power, and progression speed.

**The Real Difference:** Elf comes with racial abilities and elven flavor, while Spellblade is a pure class focused on the warrior-scholar concept without racial features.

**Balance:** The racial abilities of Elf are balanced against Spellblade's flexibility (available to humans, easier requirements for elves, no racial restrictions on stronghold/followers).

**Result:** Both are equally viable. The choice is primarily about flavor and whether you want racial abilities or pure class flexibility.
